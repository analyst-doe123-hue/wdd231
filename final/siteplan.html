National Parks Explorer

Website Plan

Site Name

National Parks Explorer

This name was selected because it clearly communicates the website's purpose - to help users explore and learn about national parks. The name is descriptive, memorable, and aligns with the content and functionality of the site.

Optional domain availability: nationalparksexplorer.org

Site Purpose

The National Parks Explorer website serves as a comprehensive digital guide to national parks, providing users with:

  • Detailed information about various national parks
  • Visual galleries showcasing park landscapes and features
  • Upcoming events and activities at different parks
  • Search and filter capabilities to find parks based on specific criteria
  • Educational content about conservation and park history

The site aims to inspire visits to national parks while providing practical information for planning trips.

Scenarios

These scenarios represent questions that potential visitors might have when accessing the site:

  1. Family Planning a Vacation: "We're planning a family vacation to a national park next month. Which parks have the best hiking trails for children ages 8-12, and what special events will be happening during our visit?"
  2. Photography Enthusiast: "I'm a landscape photographer looking to capture stunning vistas. Which national parks offer the most dramatic scenery and when is the best time of year to visit for optimal lighting conditions?"
  3. Educational Group: "Our school is organizing an educational field trip. Which parks offer guided educational programs for middle school students, and what are the reservation requirements?"
  4. Accessibility Concerns: "My mother uses a wheelchair. Which national parks have the best accessibility features and trails suitable for mobility-impaired visitors?"

Typography

The typography selection aims for readability while maintaining a connection to the natural theme of the site.

Heading Font: Roboto Slab

This serif font will be used for all headings (H1, H2, H3, etc.) to provide strong visual hierarchy and a slightly traditional, authoritative feel appropriate for educational content about national parks.

Body Font: Montserrat

This clean, modern sans-serif font will be used for all body text, providing excellent readability across devices and screen sizes while complementing the heading font.
